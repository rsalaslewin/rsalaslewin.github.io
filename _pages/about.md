Welcome! I am a PhD student in Comparative Politics at Cornell University's Department of Government. My research interests include social movements, electoral behavior, populism, and public opinion in Latin America and Southern Europe. I explore the interaction between contentious and institutional political behavior. My projects examine how individuals respond to crises of representation and how such crises reshape political cleavages. I also focus on the impact of social movements on public opinion and electoral preferences.

During the 2025-26 academic year I will be visiting the Institute of Political Science of the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile and conducting fieldwork in Chile.

At Cornell, I am a graduate fellow at the [Center on Global Democracy](https://publicpolicy.cornell.edu/cgd/). My research has also been generously supported by the [Einaudi Center for International Studies](https://einaudi.cornell.edu/discover/people/rocio-salas-lewin), the [Roper Center for Public Opinion Research](https://ropercenter.cornell.edu/staff/kohut-research-fellows), the [Cornell Center for Social Sciences (CCSS)](https://socialsciences.cornell.edu/affiliates), the [American Political Science Association (APSA)](https://apsanet.org/), and the [Center for Social Conflict and Cohesion Studies (COES)](https://coes.cl/).

Before starting my doctoral studies, I earned a MSc in Political Science and Political Economy from The London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) and a BSc in Sociology from Universidad de Chile, both with the highest distinction. I was a research assistant at the [Laboratory for the Study of the Far-Right (Ultra-Lab/UDP-PUC)](https://ultra-lab.cl/index.php), the [Chilean Longitudinal Social Survey (ELSOC-COES)](https://coes.cl/elsoc/), and the [Social Psychology Laboratory (PUC)](https://www.psicologia.uc.cl/investigacion/laboratorios/laboratorio-de-psicologia-social/).
